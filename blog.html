12 June 2026 · Seasonal stems

Why dahlia season is our favourite time of year

Dahlias only hold their shape for a few short months, which is exactly why we build half our autumn bouquets around them. Here's what we look for when buying at the farm gate: tight, unopened buds that will keep developing in the vase, stems thick enough to support the bloom's eventual weight, and colour that hasn't started to fade under the afternoon sun at the market.

3 June 2026 · Flower care

Five small habits that double your bouquet's lifespan

Most cut flowers don't fail from age — they fail from bacteria in the water. A clean vase and a daily trim go further than any flower food sachet. Re-cut each stem at an angle every two to three days, change the water fully rather than topping it up, and keep the arrangement away from direct sun and fruit bowls, which release ethylene gas that speeds up wilting.

8 May 2026 · Sustainability

Switching to fully compostable wrapping

We moved away from plastic-lined paper this year. It cost more per bouquet, but it's the kind of trade-off that matches what we actually believe — every wrap, ribbon, and water sleeve we use now breaks down within a few weeks in a home compost bin, with no microplastic left behind.
